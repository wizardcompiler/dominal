Health Checkup

Understand your workout pain - Know when to rest and when to see a doctor

Chest Pain - Center Normal

Dull ache in the center of chest after push-ups, bench press, or chest exercises

Symptoms
  • Soreness in pectoral muscles 24-48 hours after workout
  • Tenderness when touching chest muscles
  • Pain improves with rest and worsens with chest exercises
  • No shortness of breath or sweating
Why This Happens
  • Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS) from chest workouts
  • Micro-tears in pectoral muscle fibers during exercise
  • Normal response to strength training
Safe: Normal muscle soreness. Rest 48-72 hours, apply ice, and gradually return to training.
Chest Pain - Left Side Emergency

Pressure, tightness, or pain on left side of chest, especially during or after exercise

Warning Symptoms
  • Pressure, squeezing, or fullness in left chest
  • Pain radiating to left arm, jaw, neck, or back
  • Shortness of breath, sweating, nausea
  • Dizziness or light-headedness
  • Pain lasts more than a few minutes or comes and goes
Possible Causes
  • Heart attack (myocardial infarction)
  • Angina (reduced blood flow to heart)
  • Cardiac arrhythmia
EMERGENCY: Call emergency services immediately. Do not drive yourself. This could be a heart attack.
Knee Pain with "Pop" Sound See Doctor

Sudden sharp pain with audible popping during squats, lunges, or running

Red Flag Symptoms
  • Audible "pop" or "snap" sound during exercise
  • Immediate sharp pain and instability
  • Swelling within hours
  • Unable to bear weight or straighten knee
  • Knee gives out when walking
Likely Injuries
  • ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament) tear
  • Meniscus tear
  • Patellar tendon rupture
  • MCL/LCL ligament damage
See doctor immediately. Stop all exercise. Apply RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation). May require MRI and surgery.
Sharp Lower Back Pain See Doctor

Sharp, shooting pain in lower back, especially after deadlifts or heavy lifting

Warning Signs
  • Sharp, stabbing pain that radiates down legs
  • Numbness or tingling in legs or feet
  • Muscle weakness in legs
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control (emergency)
  • Pain worsens with coughing or sneezing
Possible Issues
  • Herniated disc (slipped disc)
  • Sciatica nerve compression
  • Lumbar strain or sprain
  • Spinal stenosis
See doctor within 24-48 hours. Avoid heavy lifting. May need MRI. Physical therapy often helps.
Shoulder Pain with Limited Range Monitor

Pain and difficulty lifting arm overhead after shoulder exercises

Symptoms
  • Pain when lifting arm above shoulder height
  • Weakness in shoulder movements
  • Pain at night, especially lying on affected side
  • Clicking or popping sensation
  • Difficulty reaching behind back
Possible Conditions
  • Rotator cuff tendinitis or tear
  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • Bursitis
  • Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis)
Rest 3-5 days. If no improvement or pain worsens, see doctor. May need physical therapy or imaging.
General Muscle Soreness (DOMS) Normal

Dull ache in muscles 24-72 hours after intense workout

Characteristics
  • Peaks 24-48 hours after workout
  • Dull, achy pain (not sharp)
  • Stiffness and reduced range of motion
  • Tender to touch
  • Gradually improves over 3-5 days
Science Behind It
  • Micro-tears in muscle fibers (normal adaptation)
  • Inflammation as part of muscle repair
  • Builds stronger, larger muscles
  • More common after new exercises or increased intensity
Safe and normal. Light activity, stretching, and massage help. Return to training when soreness subsides.
Ankle Swelling After Exercise Monitor

Swelling, bruising, or inability to bear weight on ankle

Warning Signs
  • Visible swelling within 2-4 hours
  • Bruising or discoloration
  • Unable to put weight on ankle
  • Ankle feels unstable or gives way
  • Severe pain when moving ankle
Possible Injuries
  • Grade 1-3 ankle sprain
  • Ligament tear (ATFL, CFL)
  • Possible fracture if very severe
  • Tendon strain
Apply RICE immediately. If unable to walk or severe swelling, see doctor within 24 hours for X-ray.
Wrist Pain During Push-ups Normal

Discomfort in wrists when bearing weight during push-ups or planks

Common Symptoms
  • Ache or pressure in wrists during weight-bearing
  • Pain goes away immediately after stopping
  • Weak wrist flexibility
  • Discomfort but no sharp pain
Why It Happens
  • Weak wrist stabilizer muscles
  • Poor wrist mobility and flexibility
  • Incorrect hand placement
  • Normal when starting push-up training
Normal for beginners. Strengthen wrists with mobility exercises. Use push-up handles or fists if needed.
Headache During Exercise Monitor

Throbbing headache during intense exercise or heavy lifting

Symptoms to Watch
  • Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache (emergency)
  • Throbbing on both sides of head
  • Headache lasts 5 minutes to 48 hours
  • Accompanied by nausea or vision changes
Possible Causes
  • Exertion headache (benign, common)
  • Dehydration
  • Holding breath during heavy lifts
  • High blood pressure (rare but serious)
Usually benign. Hydrate well, breathe properly during lifts. If sudden/severe or with vision changes, see doctor immediately.
Elbow Pain (Tendinitis) Monitor

Pain on outside or inside of elbow during bicep curls or tricep exercises

Symptoms
  • Pain on outer elbow (tennis elbow) or inner elbow (golfer's elbow)
  • Weakness in grip strength
  • Pain when lifting weights or gripping objects
  • Stiffness in the morning
  • Pain radiates into forearm
Causes
  • Overuse of forearm muscles
  • Repetitive gripping motions
  • Poor form during bicep/tricep exercises
  • Using too much weight too soon
Rest 1-2 weeks. Ice after workouts. Reduce weight and volume. If persists beyond 2 weeks, see doctor for potential physical therapy.
Shin Splints (Running Pain) Monitor

Pain along inner edge of shinbone during or after running

Symptoms
  • Dull, aching pain along inside of lower leg
  • Pain during exercise that may improve as you warm up
  • Tenderness to touch along shin
  • Mild swelling in lower leg
  • Pain worsens if continuing to run through it
Risk Factors
  • Suddenly increasing running distance or intensity
  • Running on hard surfaces
  • Worn-out or improper shoes
  • Flat feet or high arches
  • Weak ankle, hip, or core muscles
Rest from running 2-4 weeks. Ice and anti-inflammatories. Switch to low-impact cardio. If pain persists, see doctor to rule out stress fracture.
Neck Stiffness After Lifting Normal

Stiff or sore neck after overhead press, shoulder exercises, or heavy lifting

Common Symptoms
  • Muscle tightness on sides or back of neck
  • Reduced range of motion (turning head)
  • Dull ache that improves with movement
  • Tension in upper traps and neck
Why It Happens
  • Overactive upper trapezius muscles compensating
  • Poor posture during lifts (head forward)
  • Weak deep neck flexors
  • Normal muscle fatigue from stabilizing heavy loads
Normal muscle soreness. Improve form, keep neck neutral. Stretch and massage. Should resolve in 2-3 days.
Hip Flexor Pain Monitor

Pain in front of hip/groin area during squats, lunges, or running

Symptoms
  • Pain in front of hip when lifting knee
  • Discomfort during deep squats
  • Tightness in groin area
  • Pain when going upstairs or running
  • Stiffness after sitting for long periods
Common Causes
  • Hip flexor strain from overuse
  • Tight hip flexors from prolonged sitting
  • Weak glutes causing hip flexor compensation
  • Poor squat form (knees caving in)
Rest 3-7 days. Stretch hip flexors daily. Strengthen glutes. If sharp pain or clicking, see doctor to rule out labral tear.
Calf Cramps (Charlie Horse) Normal

Sudden, sharp muscle spasm in calf during or after exercise

Characteristics
  • Sudden, involuntary muscle contraction
  • Intense pain lasting seconds to minutes
  • Visible hard lump in muscle
  • Usually happens during or right after exercise
  • May occur at night after training
Common Triggers
  • Dehydration and electrolyte imbalance
  • Muscle fatigue from overtraining
  • Low potassium, magnesium, or calcium
  • Poor circulation during intense exercise
Normal and benign. Stretch calf immediately. Stay hydrated. Eat potassium and magnesium-rich foods. Warm up properly.
Abdominal Pain During Core Work Monitor

Sharp or dull pain in stomach area during crunches, planks, or heavy lifting

Different Types
  • Side stitch: Sharp pain on side during cardio (benign)
  • Ab muscle soreness: Dull ache 24-48 hours later (normal)
  • Sharp central pain with bulge: Possible hernia (see doctor)
  • Nausea with pain: May be eating too close to workout
When to Worry
  • Visible bulge or lump that doesn't go away (hernia)
  • Severe pain that prevents you from standing straight
  • Pain accompanied by fever or vomiting
  • Persistent sharp pain for more than 24 hours
Side stitch = benign (improve breathing). If visible bulge or severe pain, stop exercise and see doctor to rule out hernia.
